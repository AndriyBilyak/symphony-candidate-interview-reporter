Before you start

Remember this:

  • When interviewing you represent your company - candidates will often judge the company based on their interviewing experience
  • Remember that an interview is a two-way evaluation process - candidate is evaluating you and your company
  • You're evaluating a candidate against a benchmark, not comparing them to yourself
  • Do everyhting in your power to make the candidate comfortable. Don't get too familiar with them, but don't be too cold niether - there's a balanced polite professional stance that works well
  • What they DO know is just as important as what they DON'T - there's no single cirriculum in web development
  • Don't get too hung up on terminology. If you sense weakness - establish it's extent, make a note and move on. Don't be a bully
  • Be honest, avoid duplicity, don't be arrogant, opinionated or insultive
  • Give them a chance to tell you about something that you didn't think of asking. Maybe they know Scala or Haskell ;)
  • Try a combination of theory questions, abstract thinking (computing problems, open-ended creative problems) and a bit of practice. Get them to write a bit of code if you can
  • The best thing you could do (which is also impractical) is to let the candidate shadow you during your typical working day
